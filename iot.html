Internet of Things (IoT) and ESP8266

A simple and colorful guide for students

What is IoT?

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of everyday objects connected to the internet. These objects can collect and share data, helping us make smarter decisions and automate daily activities. IoT allows devices like watches, refrigerators, and home lights to communicate with each other through the internet.

Example: A smart bulb that turns on automatically when it gets dark, or a plant sensor that tells you when your plant needs water.

How IoT Works

Every IoT system has three main parts:

  • Sensor: Collects information (like temperature, motion, or light).
  • Controller (Microcontroller): Processes the data and sends it to the internet.
  • Cloud or App: Stores, displays, or acts on the data (like showing it on a phone or website).

Illustration: IoT connects sensors, devices, and the cloud

What is ESP8266?

The ESP8266 is a small, low-cost microcontroller chip made by Espressif Systems. It has built-in Wi-Fi, which makes it perfect for IoT projects. The ESP8266 allows your devices to send and receive data through the internet without needing an extra Wi-Fi module.

Photo: NodeMCU (ESP8266 Development Board)

The board version called NodeMCU is commonly used by students and makers. It can be easily programmed using the Arduino IDE and powered by a simple USB cable.

Why Students Love ESP8266

The ESP8266 is popular in schools and colleges because it is easy to use, affordable, and teaches important concepts about electronics, sensors, and internet communication. It helps students understand how the world is becoming smarter through technology.
